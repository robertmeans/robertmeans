Saturday, August 17, 2013 - Only 1 thing left to do this summer before school starts on Monday. Note the day packs. We're on a mission.

We're off to climb the tallest mountain in Colorado, 2nd tallest in the Continental United States (Mt. Whitney in CA is taller by 61 feet). Out the door at 4:00 a.m. On trail by 7:00 a.m.

Made treeline and first glimpse of Elbert around 8:00 a.m. That's a false peak in the background. Summit is roughly another half hour (our speed) past what you see in the distance.

Micro-recovery/acclimation break along the way.

I couldn't stop saying along the way, "See that mountain over there? We're going to be higher than that. See that mountain over there..."

We're officially the highest family in Colorado and in fact all of the Rocky Mountains.

14,433 feet high (14ers.com), 9 miles, 4,700' vertical gain and 7 hours of hiking round trip.
