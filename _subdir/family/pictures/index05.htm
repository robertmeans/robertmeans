After waiting in the truck for nearly 2 hours the rain finally broke and we got this trip underway.

I overpacked Stanton's pack so shortly into the hike I unloaded some of his heavy stuff into my pack and gave him the camera so he could document the tower of pain I'm carrying.

2.5 hour hike in landed us at a decent camping spot (hiking with headlamps) in a light drizzle, around 9:30 p.m. Here the kids wait patiently while Dad sets up home. All of our stuff was under the vestibule b/c of the rain. We had a Bushy-tailed woodrat come in and nibble on my pack that wouldn't scare away. I would shine my lamp in his face and yell (12 inches away!) and he'd go away only to return 90 seconds later. Shortly after midnight I couldn't take it anymore so I got up prepared to shoot that thing. Fortunately for him I tied everything up in bags and that was enough to keep him from eating our camping gear.

Friday hike up Fancy Pass.

This was the biggest bouquet of Columbines we've ever seen.

Summiting Fancy Pass

At the Pass we spotted a herd of big horn sheep - you can faintly make out their butts, skipping merrily single file along the scree where the green meets the rocks on the right hand side of the picture.

Taking a GORP break.

Site of Holy Cross "City" old mining town. We stumbled upon this 130 year old shack just in time for the afternoon downpour.

The last time we got caught in an afternoon storm we ended up huddled together between rocks wrapped in a trash bag. This is deluxe accommodations!

Closed off mine shaft

Another locked up mine. Imagine the miners with candels strapped to their heads crawling waaaaay down in there... *shiver*

We found a strawberry patch. Another item to add to our camp menu. They're tiny but really good!

Saddled up, moving out.
