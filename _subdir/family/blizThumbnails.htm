This is our house on the second day of the storm. The electricity went out the night before this picture (Tuesday) and didn´t come back on until Sunday morning.

When taking this picture the snow was about chest deep on me. (I´m Robert. Hi. I´m about 6´2")

These two "bumps" are our Jeeps in the driveway. It was funny at the time. Now, a week later, there´s still a serious amount of snow melt that needs to occur for us to get them back to the road. We have been catching rides into town for essentials & otherwise doing without.

There are a lot of people that are paying for front-end loaders to come & dig them out. The current rate for this service is $200.00 to show + $200.00 an hour. Others are renting cars & parking them on the road.

Because our electricity went out our only hope for heat was a fire in the woodstove. We went through the wood stacked against the house pretty quickly & then I was left shoveling a path to the woodpile (about 75 feet from the house) in the middle of the night.

I am really proud of this path. If you ever talk to me, buy me a beer and ask me about it.

Without electricity we also did not have running water. I took apart the gutter & re-routed it into a six gallon container. We were able to continue flushing the toilet by filling the back of it up each time it was used. We also used this water to heat on the stove (gas stove - horray!) and take makeshift baths at the sink.

After this experience, anything under three feet is considered flurries.
